About me (Kevin)

Hello, my name is Eugene but I personally prefer to be called by my middle name Kevin. Unlike most people who live in America, I wasn’t born here. In fact I moved a lot, country to country. I was born in Congo but was raised in Zambia for half of my life before me and my family moved to America. Being born and raised from a whole different continent, it was extremely hard for me to adjust to my new life away from everything I knew. I had to learn and master a foreign language that sounded like gibberish compared to my own. Making friends was even harder for multiple reasons, but one that stuck out to me was the realization that not everyone looked like me. Being from Africa, seeing someone white was extremely rare but once I moved here, white people were all I saw. Overall moving to america was truly life changing, I was given the opportunity to meet incredible people and experience amazing things.

I’m truly a simple man who is easily pleased by heart. All I need in life is a great movie or a great show and I will be content for days. Growing up, my widowed mother was busy providing for our family to watch me and my siblings. We also didn’t have the money or the ability to get a babysitter, so my mom would always tell me and my siblings to lock the door and not let anyone in. Us being kids and not having parents supervision, we were left to our own devices and had no other choice than to watch television. This experience brought me and my siblings closer together and it sparked my fascination for movies and shows alike. Me and my siblings would spend weeks watching television and those moments have defined me as a person. I could never trade those memories for anything. Now that we are older and with our own lives, those moments I enjoyed so much as a kid are now in the distant past. Although me and my siblings don’t watch TV together, my fascination with movies and shows is still intact. Even Though I watch by myself now, the feelings I got when watching Television with my sibling are still there and nothing brings me better joy than that feeling.

I just want to quit, I don’t wanna play soccer anymore. My passion for soccer was a bright flame that has fully been distinguished. When growing up soccer was my whole identity and without soccer I truly thought I was a nobody. Once I moved to America, my mother enrolled me into soccer as soon as I touched American soil and that's when my love for soccer began to blossom. I played soccer from 1st grade through 5th grade and those years were one of the best years of my life. I was extremely good at soccer, I even had scouts looking at me and trying to recruit me. I ended up getting recruited to a really good soccer team after 5th grade until I had to move school, that single factor changed my soccer career for the worse. My mother being a widow and the single provider for our family, she couldn’t take me to practice and I had moved too far for my old teammates to take me. In the end I ended up quitting soccer but not at my own volition. Due to that single factor I ended up not playing soccer for 3 years and when I got into high school, all the skills I had were nowhere to be found. In the end I ended up not making the team my freshman year, which crushed me and my passion for soccer slowly started fading. Even though I was crushed and sad, my hope never wavered. Thanks to that hope, I made the JV-B and JV teams during my sophomore and junior years. Those two years were moments I would never forget, those moments showed me why I loved soccer all those years ago. But as I became a senior and made varsity, the flame I once had truly disappeared. And suddenly the words “I don’t wanna play soccer anymore” came out of my mouth, which is weird because 8 year old me would have never said that.
